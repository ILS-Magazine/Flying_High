Committee Article

INNOVATION IN TEACHING AND LEARNING AT I.L.S


Teaching and learning at Indian Language School was greatly influenced by the wave of ‘innovative teaching’ that was rampant in the 21st century. Innovation in education can be defined ‘as doing things in a new way’. Innovation in education encourages students and teachers to research, explore, and use all the tools to uncover something new.


In 2020-21, the school witnessed a complete transformation from a physical teaching environment to a virtual teaching environment. Teachers and students, alike, adapted themselves to this new method, which is now the ‘new normal’. Google and Zoom classrooms soon replaced the physical classrooms, e-portfolios replaced the scrapbooks, researching replaced learning, PHET and O-labs replaced the physical school laboratories, zoom break-out rooms replaced physical discussion groups and exploration became a new norm.


Teachers used a variety of web applications to engage the online learners. Mentimeter, Miro Boards, Padlet, PearDeck, LiveWorksheets, Wordwall, NamesofWheels, Nearpod, GeoGebra, Quizziz, Quizlet and Kahoot to name a few were used to keep the learners focused and fascinated. The students found various means to represent their learning in the classroom by using Abode Spark, Animoto, Canva, Powtoon, StoryBoardthat; besides creating videos and podcasts with various other applications. The school also witnessed online proctored examinations, using the Think Merit portal, for the very first time. Some of the pedagogy strategies used Think-Group-Present; K-W-L Boards, Role-Audience-Format-Topic activity; Choice Boards; Project Based Learning; Exit tickets; S-I-T exit slips (Surprised- Interested-Troubled); Reflective thinking; Gallery Walk; Inquiry Based Learning; Brainstorming Sessions; Role-plays; Jigsaw puzzles; Virtual field trips; stimulations, collaborative learning, experiential learning, joyful learning and class debates. 


Teachers created a variety of assignments for students which involved differentiation of tasks and learning through using multiple intelligences. Thus, students were given choices to create songs, rap music, presentations with voice over modifications, nukkad nataks, historical skits, creating 3-D models and using varied of art forms.


This present pandemic situation has pushed educators and learners into taking instant leaps in their learning and teaching. Such remote learning success stories reveal the power of teacher imagination and the importance of innovation when adapting to change. The transformation was not easy . But nevertheless, the never-ending journey to empower the 21st century learners has set into motion.

Art Integrated learning is an approach to combining teaching in the classroom to outside real world projects through the medium of art, this way every student can acknowledge the true depths of certain topics which usually cannot be taught through the conventional medium of book and paper. Through this tough year of Covid-19 all routine methods of learning have been left behind and a small era of online learning has taken over art integration projects which makes learning and teaching more joyful.


Now as we say "actions speak louder then words" so our committee to start off, made a short video demonstrating how art can be integrated into all topics of learning from all junior grades to senior. Our most significant project was to help students who weren’t familiar with the online learning applications, a series of webinars were hosted to teach on how to operate certain applications such as Google drive, Padlet, Quizizz, Infographic, Canva and Edunext.


A variety of ideas were shared as to how doing projects broadens the horizon of understanding for all students. The webinars held were highly successful and it helped the students a lot with their daily activities of learning in the classroom integrating ICT. In the end I would like to sum up in the words of this powerful quote:


"Art has the power to transform to illuminate, to educate, inspire and motivate."
