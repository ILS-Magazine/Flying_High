Principal's Message

This year has been a very unusual year for all the mankind globally. The COVID- 19 pandemic has transformed the way we behave, live and think. It has taught us that nothing is more important than our health, well-being and safety. I want to congratulate all the stakeholders on your patience, perseverance and adapting to the new normal.


The journey of frog leaping from face-to-face class to an online methodology of Teaching and Learning has not be an easy one. The school has tried its best to keep the education on track, emotionally support the students and find ways to help them in these unprecedented times. The school has organized several webinars, session and workshops in diverse areas to give every exposure that a child could have got if they were physically present in school.


I strongly encourage everyone to connect using the many technology options available as connectivity is critical and prime during such times when most of us feel disturbed and depressed. With this connectivity we can bring a little humanity back into our daily lives.


The school has reopened in a limited way and we have conducted the Preliminary Examination for the grades X and XII with the minimum staff on the school campus. Proper Social distancing is being observed in and around the school.


With all of us getting vaccinated for COVID by the mid or this year or little later we are going to look behind us and say, itâ€™s over and here we are together standing strong as ever and back at Indian Language School.


Take care! I look forward to seeing you all in person.

Being elected as Head Boy of Indian Language School was something I dreamt of since my early years in school. And what a year my fate decided to grant those wishes.


2020 has proven to be a year with a difference. We cannot reflect on this year without mentioning the Covid-19 pandemic and all its effects.  It has taught us patience, love, and care for the less fortunate. It has also taught us how to be stronger yet kinder. This pandemic period has also shown how helpless and insignificant we are. None of us would have expected things to have played out this way, but at the end of it, here we are.
